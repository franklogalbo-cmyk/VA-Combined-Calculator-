VA Combined Rating Calculator

Implements 38 CFR § 4.25 (combined ratings), § 4.26 (bilateral factor), and § 4.26(d) (most-favorable scenario testing). Full arithmetic precision is carried between every combination step — no intermediate rounding — per the whole-person method. The bilateral-adjusted value is rounded to the nearest whole number before final combination. The final rating is rounded to the nearest 10 % per § 4.25, with values ending in 5 rounding up to the next 10.

How to use:
Enter ratings as comma-separated whole-number percentages, e.g. 30, 20, 10.
  • Left / Right Upper Extremity — arms, shoulders, elbows, wrists, hands
  • Left / Right Lower Extremity — hips, thighs, knees, ankles, feet
  • Other Ratings — all non-extremity or non-bilateral service-connected disabilities
§ 4.26(d) logic applied:
1. All compensable paired extremity combinations are generated (powerset).
2. Each valid subset is classified as upper-only, lower-only, or combined upper + lower.
3. The bilateral factor (10 % of the combined bilateral value) is applied once per subset.
4. Excluded extremity ratings are combined separately with other ratings.
5. The scenario producing the highest final rating is selected as the most favorable result.
